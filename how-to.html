A beginners guide

What is "RC"?

Welcome to the fascinating realm of “hobby-class” radio controlled cars, trucks and boats. Hobby-class RC vehicles, unlike 'toy-class’ vehicles, are engineered for maximum performance with sophisticated suspension systems and power trains. RC models are also made incredibly durable to stand up to the extra abuse that results from their higher performance capabilities. RC vehicles can reach speeds faster than 100 mph and have fully adjustable suspension systems that operate just like their full-size counterparts. RC models are designed to be serviced and repaired by the owner with basic hand tools. The materials used in manufacturing RC vehicles are very durable and can withstand many crashes or rollovers. Replacement parts are also available through hobby stores and hobby mail order stores in case something does happen to break. There are many types of hobby-class vehicles available to accommodate a variety of different budgets and skill levels. Your hobby dealer can help you find the right model for your skill level and budget.

Build vs. Buy

Ultimately, it`s up to you and your comfort level. RTR(Ready to Run) RC units are great for beginners and low on maintenance. They are ready right out of the box the most you may need to do is charge the battery. It has everything you need and can upgrade at anytime. The downside of RTR, is that you dont get the to learn all the ins and outs about what makes your vehicle run. If something breaks you know just how to fix it and put it back together. When you buy a kit, you`re buying the parts and building your vehicle from the ground up. You`re able to customize every single screw and bolt. On the other side, it requires tons of time to put together. Most kits come with limited pieces and require you to buy them seperate such as motors, batteries, tires, body paint, etc.

What kind of RC should I get?

It can be tough to decide on what radio control vehicle is right for you. There are so many manufacturers and different types of cars, trucks, and buggies that finding the best product for your application can seem nearly impossible.

Commonly used tools

  • Long, thin flat-blade screwdriver
  • Medium needle nose pliers

Tools for nitro accessories

  • Fuel bottle
  • Glow plug wrench
  • 8 AA batteries for the radio system
  • After-run oil
  • 7.2V battery(s) and charger(for the EZ-START system)

Tools for electric accessories

  • 4 AA batteries for the radio system
  • 8.4V battery pack
  • Charger for 8.4V battery pack
